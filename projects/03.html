Sales Channel Analysis for a New Office Stationery Product Line

Introduction

In today’s competitive market, businesses must constantly innovate and refine their sales strategies to stay ahead. Our company recently launched a new line of office stationery designed to spark creativity and enhance brainstorming sessions. To identify the most effective sales strategy, we tested three approaches: Email outreach, Phone calls, and a combination of both (Email + Call).

This project analyzes the performance of these sales methods over a six-week period, providing data-driven insights to optimize our sales efforts. By leveraging Python for data cleaning, analysis, and visualization, and introducing a custom metric to measure sales efficiency, I uncovered actionable recommendations to drive the success of our new product line.

Problem Statement

The goal of this analysis was to evaluate the effectiveness of three sales methods - Email, Call, and Email + Call in driving revenue and customer engagement. Specifically, I aimed to answer the following questions:

  • Which sales method reaches the most customers?
  • How does revenue vary across sales methods?
  • Which method generates the highest revenue per unit of effort?
  • What are the long-term trends in revenue for each method?
  • How do customer engagement metrics (e.g., site visits, purchases) differ across methods?

By addressing these questions, I sought to identify the most efficient and sustainable sales strategy for our new product line.

Data Cleaning & Transformation

The dataset consisted of 15,000 rows and 8 columns, capturing details such as sales method, revenue, customer ID, and engagement metrics. Here’s how I prepared the data for analysis:

  • Standardizing Sales Methods: The sales_method column initially contained inconsistent values (e.g., "em + call" and "email"). I mapped these to three standardized values: Email, Call, and Email + Call.
  • Handling Missing Revenue Data: There were 1,074 missing values in the revenue column. I replaced these with the mean revenue for each respective sales method.
  • Correcting Invalid Data: Two entries in the years_as_customer column exceeded the company’s founding year (1984). I corrected these to the maximum possible value of 39 years.
  • Checking for Duplicates: The dataset had no duplicate rows, ensuring the integrity of the analysis.

After cleaning, the dataset was ready for exploratory analysis.

Defining a Key Business Metric: Average Revenue per Customer Sales Effort (ARPSE)

To evaluate the efficiency of each sales method, I introduced a custom metric called Average Revenue per Customer Sales Effort (ARPSE). This metric accounts for the effort required for each method, providing a balanced comparison of performance.

The formula for ARPSE is:

ARPSE = Total Revenue for Method / (Number of Customers × Sales Effort)

Where:

  • Email has a sales effort of 0.5 (low effort).
  • Email + Call has a sales effort of 1 (moderate effort).
  • Call has a sales effort of 3 (high effort).
            
              # Define the sales effort for each sales method
              sales_effort = {
                'Email': 0.5,
                'Call': 3,
                'Email + Call': 1
              }

              # Group the data by sales_method and aggregate the total revenue and number of customers
              grouped_data = sales_data.groupby('sales_method').agg({'revenue': 'sum', 'customer_id': 'count'}).reset_index()

              # Calculate ARPSE for each sales method
              grouped_data['ARCPSE'] = grouped_data.apply(lambda row: row['revenue'] / (row['customer_id'] * sales_effort[row['sales_method']]), axis=1)

              # Display the ARPSE for each sales method
              print(grouped_data[['sales_method', 'ARCPSE']])

Key Insights

  • Email + Call Drives Higher Engagement: This method leads to more purchases and site visits, indicating stronger customer engagement.
  • Email is Scalable but Declines Over Time: While Email reaches the most customers, its revenue declines over time, suggesting diminishing returns.
  • Call is Less Efficient: Despite generating strong initial revenue, the Call method is the least efficient and shows a downward trend.

Recommendations

Based on the analysis, I recommend the following actions:

  • Prioritize Email + Call for Long-Term Growth: This method drives higher engagement and consistent revenue growth, making it the most sustainable option.
  • Use Email for Scalability: Leverage Email to reach a broad audience, but complement it with other methods to maintain revenue.
  • Reevaluate the Call Method: Given its low efficiency and declining revenue, consider reducing reliance on this method or improving its effectiveness.
