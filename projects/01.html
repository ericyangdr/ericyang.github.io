Coffee Shop Sales Dashboard: Brewing Insights from Data

Background

In today’s data-driven world, understanding customer behavior and sales trends is crucial for any business. This project dives into the sales data of a coffee shop chain, uncovering key insights to drive growth and efficiency. Using Power BI, I transformed raw data into actionable insights, creating a three-page interactive dashboard that answers critical business questions.

The Dataset

The dataset contained transactional data from January to June 2023, including:

  • Transaction details: Date, time, quantity, and unit price.
  • Store information: Location and store ID.
  • Product details: Category, type, and specific product information.

This data was the foundation for uncovering trends in sales, customer behavior, and product performance.

Data Cleaning and Transformation (ETL)

Before diving into analysis, I cleaned and transformed the data using Power Query:

  • Removed duplicates and handled missing values.
  • Extracted key features like day of the week, month, and hour from the transaction timestamp.
  • Created calculated columns like Total Sales (transaction_qty * unit_price) and Growth Rate.

This step ensured the data was accurate and ready for analysis.

Building the Dashboard

The dashboard is divided into three pages, each focusing on a specific aspect of the business:

Page 1: Sales Overview

  • Key KPIs: Total Sales, Total Quantity Sold, and Total Transactions.
  • Visualizations:
    • Pie charts for Sales by Store and Quantity by Store.
    • Column chart for Sales by Month with an average sales line.
    • Bar chart for Sales by Product Category with an average sales line.
    • Table chart for Product Category Details (sales, % grand total, unit price, quantity sold).

Page 2: Monthly Sales

  • Key KPIs: Monthly Total Sales, Total Quantity Sold, and Total Transactions with Growth Rate vs. the previous month.
  • Visualizations:
    • Drill-down bar chart for Monthly Sales by Product Category (Category → Type → Detail).
    • Column chart for Sales by Day.
    • Table chart for Product Category Performance (current month sales, previous month sales, growth rate).

Page 3: Customer Behavior

  • Visualizations:
    • Heatmaps:
      • Calendar heatmap to identify the busiest days.
      • Time vs. Day heatmap to pinpoint peak sales hours (6 AM–8 PM).
    • Scatter plot: Average Unit Price vs. Average Quantity Sold.
    • Top 5 and Bottom 5 Products: Column charts comparing current and previous month sales with drill-down functionality.

Key Findings

Here are the key insights uncovered from the analysis:

Sales Performance

  • Total Sales: $699K, with Coffee leading at 38% of total sales.
  • Top Stores: Hell’s Kitchen contributed 34%+ of total sales.
  • Monthly Trends: Sales dipped in February but rebounded in March (+29.8%) and grew steadily in June (+6.2%).

Customer Behavior

  • Peak Sales Times: 7 AM–11 AM (morning rush) and 5 PM (evening rush).
  • Product Trends:
    • Best-Sellers: Cappuccino (Large), Dark Chocolate (Large), Latte (Regular).
    • Worst-Sellers: Dark Chocolate, Earl Grey, English Breakfast.

Opportunities for Growth

  • Focus on promoting best-selling products during peak hours.
  • Improve marketing for underperforming products like teas and specialty items.
  • Optimize staffing and inventory during peak sales periods.

Recommendations

Based on the findings, here are my recommendations:

  1. Leverage Peak Hours: Increase staffing and stock during 7 AM–11 AM and 5 PM to maximize sales.
  2. Promote Best-Sellers: Highlight top-performing products like Cappuccino and Latte through targeted marketing campaigns.
  3. Revamp Underperforming Products: Consider rebranding or discontinuing low-performing items like Earl Grey and English Breakfast.
  4. Store-Specific Strategies: Tailor strategies for high-performing stores like Astoria and Hell’s Kitchen to replicate success across other locations.

Conclusion

This project was a fantastic learning experience, teaching me how to turn raw data into actionable insights. By analyzing sales trends, customer behavior, and product performance, I was able to identify opportunities for growth and efficiency.
